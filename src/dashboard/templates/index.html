    INFORMATION INTEGRITY / POLLUTION DEFENSE Claim-first verification · live in production via tier-1/2 corroboration logic · §10.13 + §10.15
    • Social/community data may indicate attention — it cannot, by itself, confirm a thesis.
    • Primary/reputable-source corroboration is required before any claim is allowed to support a trade thesis (Tier 1 = SEC/regulator/issuer; Tier 2 = WSJ/Reuters/Bloomberg/FT).
    • High attention + low credibility = pollution risk, not alpha. A spike in mentions without a primary-source anchor is a risk flag.
    • Bot / coordinated-campaign / rumor risk must be flagged — `coordinated_campaign_warning`, `bot_like_pattern_risk`, `rumor_without_evidence_flag` are agent-emitted descriptors.
    • Sentiment is a descriptor, not a rule. No "negative sentiment ⇒ short" or "positive sentiment ⇒ buy" rule exists in this system, today or in any frozen-rules version.
    • If only T4/T5 evidence exists, `use_as_primary_signal_allowed = false`. Agent collapses to `needs_more_evidence` or `pollution_risk_high`.
